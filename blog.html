THE 8 DUMBEST WAYS OUR BRAINS SCREWS US OVER
AND HOW TO WIN BECAUSE OF IT

AUTHOR:
NIKOLAJ STORM PETERSEN

01 THIS IS MY TOY!

We’re scared little babies about losing stuff. This is more formaly known as loss aversion. We feel losses way harder than gains, think 2x the gut punch. I once held onto a crypto coin that tanked because selling felt like admitting defeat. Kahneman and Tversky showed we’d rather gamble than cut losses. Lesson: Cut the cord, dude.

“Silence itself was a revolution. Absence became louder than presence.”

02 FRAMING EFFECT

Words are sneaky bastards. “75% fat-free” yogurt sounds like health food, but “25% fat” sounds like a heart attack. Kahneman and Tversky proved we’ll pick surgery with a “90% survival rate” over “10% death rate,” even though it’s identical. Lesson: Read the fine print, ignore the hype.

03 ENDOWMENT EFFECT

My junk is my treasure. I refused to sell an old skateboard for $50 because it was mine, but I wouldn’t pay $20 for it on eBay. Richard Thaler showed owners demand more to sell than they’d pay to buy. Lesson: Your stuff isn’t that special. Let it go.

“The unfinished was the finished. The raw was the refined.”

04 ANCHORING BIAS

The first number screws you. I overpaid for a laptop because the tag said “was $1200, now $800.” Felt like a deal, but worth maybe $600. Even random numbers can anchor our guesses. Lesson: Ignore the first number, it’s a mental trap designed to mess with you.
My grandpa got this one right. He used to say "it's cheap if it's worth more". And that's the key, look at utility

05 SUNK COST FALLACY

Throwing good money after bad. I stuck with a shady sales job because I’d already put in months. That’s sunk cost fallacy—doubling down on losses. Thaler says we hate wasting past effort. It’s why people stay in dead-end relationships. Lesson: Past costs are gone. Focus on what’s next.

06 PRESENT BIAS

Gimme now, screw tomorrow. I blew savings on a leather jacket instead of rent. That’s present bias—instant gratification over future gains. Laibson showed we’d take $100 today over $125 in a year. Lesson: Trick yourself into saving, automate decisions so your future self doesn’t hate you.

07 STATUS QUO BIAS

Change is for suckers. Ever stick with a crappy phone plan because switching feels like a hassle? That’s status quo bias. Madrian showed default 401(k) opt-outs skyrocket enrollment. We resist change from fear or laziness. Lesson: Staying stuck is worse than switching. Rip the Band-Aid off.

08 OVERCONFIDENCE BIAS

I’m a freaking genius (spoiler: I’m not). I thought I could nail a pitch with zero prep. Crashed and burned. Gruber says 80% of drivers think they’re above average (impossible!). Overconfidence blinds us to risk. Lesson: Humble up, plan better, and assume you’re wrong sometimes.
